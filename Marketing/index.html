Information

Really Simple takes RSS feeds from various quality news sources and displays them in an easy to read way, optimised for the iPhone/iTouch. The design and type is intended to be as easy on the eye as printed text.

Really Simple saves all the news feeds and images for reading offline. You can pick a selection of 8 different categories, each source is periodically updated throughout the day.

Features

  • High quality news sources with mobile-friendly text.
  • Offline news reading for the plane, tube etc.
  • Only the freshest news is kept - save yourself trawling through 100s of old news feeds.
  • Download images while you read.
  • Fast and compact format - no lengthy waits for large RSS feeds.
  • Pick the categories only you're interested in.

Troubleshooting

The app says "Getting latest feeds" and then does nothing

Please check your internet connection isn't slow/flakey before getting the latest feeds (WIFI is recommended if you want images as well). If you are using 3G or Edge then try increasing the time out in the settings. Also ensure that your device is not connected via USB to your Mac or PC as this can cause issues.

I choose a category and it says "There are no new items available right now."

This can be because of one of three reasons:

  1. Your settings have "hide read items" set, and you have read all the items for the categories you have chosen.
  2. You haven't updated before, and your internet connection is slow/flakey so Really Simple wasn't able to update the feeds.
  3. You've updated, but the categories really don't have any new news items! This will very rarely happen however.

Download images is enabled, but I don't see any images

The first few images for the categories you have chosen are initially downloaded when the feeds are updated. The remaining images are then downloaded in the background (the pulser denotes this on the toolbar). If you do not see any images, give the app a few minutes to finish downloading the images and then they will appear.

Some of the images are stretched and distorted

Unfortunately not all the feeds have thumbnail images designed for mobile device - a few use images that are small. Future versions of the app will cater for this.
